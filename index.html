What is EMP?

EMP, or Electro-Mechanical Production, refers to the manufacturing process that combines electrical and mechanical components to create finished products. This interdisciplinary approach involves the integration of electronic systems, sensors, actuators, and mechanical structures to design and produce devices with enhanced functionality and efficiency. EMP is commonly employed in various industries, including robotics, automation, and mechatronics, where the synergy of electrical and mechanical elements is crucial for the development of advanced and sophisticated products.

FAQs

What is Electro-Mechanical Production?

EMP represents the integration of electrical and mechanical engineering. This program emphasizes expertise in the technological aspects associated with the design, maintenance, and operation of electromechanical systems, specifically automation and robotics. Such systems encompass electronic, mechanical, instrumentation, and control elements.

Why do we study EMP?

Electro-Mechanical Technology (or mechatronics) is one of the most in-demand and highly sought-after careers in the manufacturing industry. The combination of electrical and mechanical skills based on computer-aided drafting, and machine technology creates a unique blend of knowledge desirable to the industry.

EMP in daily life

It is used to move objects, create conveniences, and help us achieve what we cannot do with human power alone. From hydropower to wind power to the machines that make the machines that generate electricity, mechanical energy is not only present but it's the driving force

How do EMP concepts blend with engineering?

EMP merges electrical and mechanical knowledge, exploring modern machines and their components for a holistic understanding in engineering..
